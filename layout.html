Multiple Job-Holders in Australia

Overview

More Australians than ever are holding multiple jobs. This project visualises national and state-level data to show how multiple job-holding has evolved over time, and how it varies by gender and industry. The goal is to highlight key insights about Australia's changing workforce.

Trend Over Time

The rate remained relatively stable between 1994 and 2019, fluctuating around 5.5-6%, before a large decline in June 2020 caused by the Covid 19 pandemic. Since then, the rate has climbed notably.

Multiple Job-Holding by Sex

Women consistently hold multiple jobs at higher rates than men throughout the entire 30-year period.Females average around 6.5-7%, while males stay closer to 4.5-5%.

Map: Prevalence by State

The map above shows the regional variation in job-holding rates across territories. In June 2025, the multiple job-holding rate was highest in Greater Hobart (capital city) and Tasmania (state) and lowest in Sydney and Queensland.

Multiple Job-Holding by Industry

Industry variation shows significant differences in multiple job-holding rates, with some sectors showing much higher propensity for workers to hold multiple jobs.
